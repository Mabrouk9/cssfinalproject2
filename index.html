Gray wolf.

The gray wolf
(Canis lupus) is the largest wild member of the dog family (Canidae), native to much of the Northern Hemisphere. Highly adaptable and social, it is recognized for its intelligence, cooperative hunting, and iconic howling that helps coordinate pack activity and mark territory. Once widespread, its populations now survive primarily in North America, Europe, and Asia

The Arctic is the northernmost polar region of Earth, encompassing the Arctic Ocean and parts of eight countries above roughly 66.5° N latitude. Characterized by extreme cold, vast ice cover, and distinctive daylight cycles, it is both a critical climate regulator and home to diverse wildlife and Indigenous cultures. The region is increasingly significant for climate research, resource exploration, and international cooperation.

Canada is a country in North America known for its vast natural landscapes, multicultural society, and stable democratic governance. It spans the northern half of the continent, sharing the world's longest undefended border with the United States, and ranks among the wealthiest nations globally.

Greenland is the world's largest island, an autonomous territory within the Kingdom of Denmark, located in the North Atlantic and Arctic Oceans. Known for its vast ice sheet and Inuit heritage, it holds strategic geopolitical importance and is increasingly significant in discussions about climate change and Arctic development.

Caribou
The caribou (Rangifer tarandus), also known as the reindeer in Eurasia, is a large hoofed mammal in the deer family (Cervidae). It inhabits Arctic and subarctic regions across North America, Europe, and Asia, where it is uniquely adapted to cold, snowy environments. Caribou are ecologically vital grazers and central to the cultural traditions of many northern Indigenous peoples.

Muskox
The muskox (Ovibos moschatus) is a large, shaggy-coated hoofed mammal native to Arctic regions of North America and Greenland. Known for its thick coat and strong odor emitted by males during the rut, it is one of the Arctic's most iconic tundra herbivores, well adapted to extreme cold and scarce vegetation.
